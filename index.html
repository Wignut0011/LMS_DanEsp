THE BEGINNING

Superbase was created by John Base. John Base was born on the 25th of July 1982. He had his first success in 2003 with his university newspaper "House". "House" was published every two weeks during the academic year, with a total of 36 issues. He majored in design, but failed to get a job in the field due to him being born without thumbs. This was a blow for him, and this is when he first got the idea for Superbase.

"Oi! You don' have thumbs, mate. 'ow are you gonna draw buildings without them thumbs?"

Supebase was inspired on a nice summer evening in 1958. John Base was walking home after his shift at the horse-glue factory, when he witnessed an old lady chasing a crying man in a Baseball uniform while screaming. The sheer insanity of what he was witnessing was indescribable. How did this happen? Why did this happen? Why was this man wearing a baseball uniform? This prompted John to explore how a game can combine social immersion, baseballs, and a healthy amount of tears.

THE GAME

While Superbase might look like ordinary Baseball on the surface, it plays much quicker due to the homing missiles. The ball is dead, so it is the batter's objective to reach second base while shooting those missiles to reach home. Do not be surprised if the bullets find an unexpected route! The faster the team scores, the more damage they can do to their opponents. When the batter hit's a home run, he "gets away" with more danger. After his "stolen base", there are two more chances to reach home with more explosions.
